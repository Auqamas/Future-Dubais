Understanding UAE’s Corporate Tax Law: The Key to Financial Compliance

The UAE’s Corporate Tax Law (Federal Decree-Law No. 47 of 2022), in effect for financial years starting on or after June 1, 2023, requires businesses to keep precise financial records and comply with International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). This move enhances transparency in financial reporting, ensuring businesses are prepared for tax assessments and in full compliance with regulatory expectations.

 The Federal Tax Authority (FTA) has underscored the importance of timely compliance, with penalties outlined in Cabinet Decision No. 75 of 2023 for non-compliance. Businesses, particularly those with new licenses issued in recent months, must register for corporate tax by the set deadlines to avoid fines. This law highlights the critical role of accurate accounting and record-keeping for maintaining regulatory compliance and sustaining long-term business success in the UAE.

Essential Accounting and Bookkeeping Compliance for Dubai Businesses

In Dubai, businesses are required to maintain precise and up-to-date financial records in accordance with the Federal Law No. 2 of 2015 on Commercial Companies (CCL). This regulation mandates companies to prepare annual financial statements that adhere to International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS), ensuring consistency and transparency in financial reporting. Compliance with UAE VAT law further emphasizes the need for detailed transaction records and timely VAT filings, making meticulous bookkeeping practices essential.
